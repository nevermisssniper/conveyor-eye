# ConveyorEye — Architecture Reference

Last updated: **2026-06-28**

---

## Stack

YOLOv8n (3.2M params) fine-tuned from COCO on 2 000 synthetic images, then
domain-adapted to MVTec AD Tile. Python 3.10 / PyTorch / Ultralytics. Inference
target: Mac CPU (local) and Colab T4/L4 GPU.

---

## YOLOv8n Layer Map

All 23 layers (index 0–22). "Frozen" means the layer is frozen by
`model.train(freeze=N)` which locks layers `0..N-1`.

| idx | type   | in→out ch     | stride | semantic role                         | freeze=4 | freeze=10 |
|-----|--------|---------------|--------|---------------------------------------|----------|-----------|
| 0   | Conv   | 3 → 16        | 2      | stem — RGB edges                      | frozen   | frozen    |
| 1   | Conv   | 16 → 32       | 2      | low-level blobs                       | frozen   | frozen    |
| 2   | C2f    | 32 → 32       | 1      | shallow bottleneck                    | frozen   | frozen    |
| 3   | Conv   | 32 → 64       | 2      | early spatial downscale               | frozen   | frozen    |
| 4   | C2f    | 64 → 64       | 1      | mid texture (P2)                      | **trains** | frozen  |
| 5   | Conv   | 64 → 128      | 2      | —                                     | **trains** | frozen  |
| 6   | C2f    | 128 → 128     | 1      | mid texture (P3)                      | **trains** | frozen  |
| 7   | Conv   | 128 → 256     | 2      | —                                     | **trains** | frozen  |
| 8   | C2f    | 256 → 256     | 1      | deep semantics (P4/P5)                | **trains** | frozen  |
| 9   | SPPF   | 256 → 256     | 1      | multi-scale pooling (backbone out)    | **trains** | frozen  |
| 10  | Upsample | 256 → 256  | ×2     | FPN up                                | **trains** | **trains** |
| 11  | Concat | —             | —      | FPN lateral (→ layer 6)               | **trains** | **trains** |
| 12  | C2f    | 384 → 128     | 1      | neck P4                               | **trains** | **trains** |
| 13  | Upsample | 128 → 128  | ×2     | FPN up                                | **trains** | **trains** |
| 14  | Concat | —             | —      | FPN lateral (→ layer 4)               | **trains** | **trains** |
| 15  | C2f    | 192 → 64      | 1      | neck P3 (small-obj head feed)         | **trains** | **trains** |
| 16  | Conv   | 64 → 64       | 2      | PAN down                              | **trains** | **trains** |
| 17  | Concat | —             | —      | PAN lateral (→ layer 12)             | **trains** | **trains** |
| 18  | C2f    | 192 → 128     | 1      | neck P4 (med-obj head feed)           | **trains** | **trains** |
| 19  | Conv   | 128 → 128     | 2      | PAN down                              | **trains** | **trains** |
| 20  | Concat | —             | —      | PAN lateral (→ layer 9)              | **trains** | **trains** |
| 21  | C2f    | 384 → 256     | 1      | neck P5 (large-obj head feed)         | **trains** | **trains** |
| 22  | Detect | 64/128/256    | —      | decoupled head (box + cls × 5)        | **trains** | **trains** |

**Why freeze=10 failed for real-world transfer:** layers 4, 6, 8 (the mid/deep
texture C2f blocks) stayed frozen at COCO+synthetic weights, so the model had no
way to discriminate real MVTec crack texture from clean tile.

**Why freeze=4 should work:** layers 4–9 are now trainable. These are where
texture-specific representations form. 230 real good tiles give gradient signal
showing what "not crack" looks like in real domain.

## Data Lineage

```
COCO (pretrained)
  └─ train.py (50 ep, lr0=0.005) — 2 000 synthetic frames
       └─ runs/conveyoreye/weights/best.pt   [mAP@50 = 0.989]
            └─ finetune.py round 1 (20 ep, freeze=10, AdamW) — +17 real crack
            │    └─ runs/conveyoreye_ft/weights/best.pt  [ft1: FPR 100%]
            │
            └─ finetune.py round 2 (30 ep, freeze=4, SGD) — +17 crack + 230 good
                 └─ runs/conveyoreye_ft2/weights/best.pt  [target: FPR < 50%]
```

### Synthetic data (`data/raw/`)
Generated by `scripts/simulate_data.py --n-train 2000 --n-val 400 --seed 0`.
Deterministic. 640×640 BGR uint8, YOLO-format labels. Class priors intentionally
imbalanced: scratch 40% / crack 22% / dent 18% / discoloration 12% / missing_part 8%.

### Real data (`data/real/`)
Source: MVTec AD Tile dataset. Not committed (gitignored); must be prepped locally.

| split | source | count | label |
|-------|--------|-------|-------|
| `real/images/crack` | MVTec tile/test/crack | 17 | class 1, full-image box |
| `real/images/good`  | MVTec tile/train/good | 230 | empty (background negative) |

`scripts/convert_mvtec_labels.py` — converts MVTec mask → YOLO bbox for crack images.
`scripts/prep_real_good.py` — copies 230 good tiles and writes empty label files.

### finetune.yaml
`path: ..` resolves to the repo root from the yaml's location (`data/finetune.yaml`),
so absolute paths are never needed. Works identically on Mac and Colab.

## Training Phases

| phase | script | from | data | freeze | optimizer | lr0 | epochs | result |
|-------|--------|------|------|--------|-----------|-----|--------|--------|
| initial | train.py | yolov8n.pt (COCO) | 2 000 synth | 0 | auto | 0.005 | 50 | mAP@50 0.989 |
| ft1 | finetune.py | conveyoreye/best.pt | synth + 17 crack | 10 | AdamW (auto) | — | 20 (stopped ep7) | FPR 100% |
| ft2 | finetune.py | conveyoreye/best.pt | synth + 17 crack + 230 good | 4 | SGD | 0.0002 | 30 | target FPR < 50% |

Notes:
- ft1 used `optimizer='auto'` which ignored `--lr0`; AdamW chose its own LR. Lesson: always set `optimizer='SGD'` when you want lr0 to be respected.
- ft2 restarts from the original `conveyoreye/best.pt` (not ft1), since ft1 degraded and there's no point warm-starting from a broken checkpoint.

## Inference Pipeline

```
frame (H×W×3 BGR uint8)
  └─ preprocess_frame()
       letterbox → 640×640  +  /255  →  CHW float32
       stores (scale, pad_x, pad_y) for box reversal
       └─ Detector.predict()          [conveyoreye/model/detector.py]
            YOLO forward → raw (N, 4+1+5) tensors
            NMS (IoU 0.50, conf 0.25)
            └─ boxes (N,4 xyxy px) · conf (N,) · cls (N,) ∈ {0..4}
                 └─ ConfidenceCalibrator.calibrate(cls, conf)   [optional]
                      per-class operating threshold (configs/thresholds.yaml)
                           └─ DetectionResult{ Detection[], latency_ms, frame_id }
```

## Eval Pipeline

Two separate evaluators — do not conflate:

**Synthetic val** (`scripts/evaluate.py`):
- 400 synthetic frames, YOLO-format labels
- Greedy IoU@0.5 match → per-class P/R/F1 + mAP@50
- Calibration fit (temperature / Platt / isotonic) → ECE
- Failure taxonomy → FP/FN type breakdown
- Run after every checkpoint to catch regression

**Real-world eval** (`scripts/eval_mvtec.py`):
- MVTec tile test set: 17 crack + N good tiles
- Binary metric: crack recall + good FPR
- No mAP — just "did it fire on crack, did it not fire on good"
- ft1 baseline: 100% recall, 100% FPR (useless)
- ft2 target: recall ≥ 85%, FPR < 50%

## Config Files

| file | controls |
|------|----------|
| `configs/thresholds.yaml` | per-class operating thresholds; asymmetric (crack recall-biased, missing_part precision-biased) |
| `configs/thresholds.generated.yaml` | eval-only overrides from threshold sweep — crack 0.03, missing_part 0.01; do not use for production until recalibrated on ft2 |
| `configs/augmentation.yaml` | aug hyperparams with physical rationale (why no rotation: conveyor is horizontal) |
| `data/finetune.yaml` | fine-tune dataset sources; `path: ..` = repo root |
| `data/raw/dataset.yaml` | synthetic-only training; generated by simulate_data.py |
